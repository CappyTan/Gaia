# ADR 0008 — Seamless, continuous overworld (no-load region roaming)

**Status:** accepted (Aurelion build, staged). Builds on [ADR 0005](0005-modular-ts-vite.md) (layering),
[ADR 0006](0006-explorable-settlements-greenfield-zones.md) (data-driven bespoke zones), and
[ADR 0007](0007-versioned-save-resume.md) (save state). Sets the direction the **world-cartographer**
([`../../.claude/agents/world-cartographer.md`](../../.claude/agents/world-cartographer.md)) frames and
the level-designer builds into.

## Context

Today the overworld is a set of **discrete zones**: each is generated by `field.genMap()` into its own
grid, the camera scrolls *within* it, and moving to another zone is a hard transition — a boss-gated
hub chain → `loadZone()` → an overlay → a fresh map. The player always knows they've "changed zones."

Dara's direction: make the world **continuous and seamless**. Roam across a region's border — in any
direction, **including diagonally** (e.g. wander *southwest* into a new region) — with **no load, no
overlay, no discrete transition**. The geography and biome change as you cross (Greenvale's shire gives
way to Silverwood's old growth), region tilesets **interlock intricately** at the seam rather than
meeting at a hard edge, and the "zoning" is **invisible to the player** — it's one world.

## Decision

**1 · One shared WORLD-SPACE.** Regions stop being independent grids and become tiles of a single
continuous map. The **world-cartographer** owns placement: each region occupies a rectangle in a shared
world-coordinate frame, positioned to match the **world atlas**, with neighbors **contiguous along
shared borders** and **8-direction adjacency** (N/S/E/W **and diagonals**). World position — not a
"current zone index" — is the source of truth.

**2 · Render in world-space; stream neighbors at the edges.** The camera follows the player in world
coordinates and draws **every region overlapping the viewport** — so as you approach a border the
neighboring region is already on screen, and crossing it shows both regions at once. No blank, no
reload. Only viewport-near regions are generated/kept (cull/stream the rest) to stay light on
GitHub Pages + iOS Safari.

**3 · Seams blend, they don't cut.** Where two regions meet, the tilesets **transition over a blend
band** (interleaved/dithered biome tiles, aligned paths crossing the border) so the change reads as
*geography shifting*, not a wall between zones. The cartographer aligns the borders (a road leaving
Greenvale's north edge meets Silverwood's south edge at the same world-y); the level-designer composes
the tiles up to and across the seam.

**4 · Region state is derived from position.** The "current region" is a function of world position;
**encounters, biome, ambient music, and difficulty** are sampled from where you *are* and shift
smoothly as you cross (music cross-fades, encounter tables blend at the border). No `loadZone`
ceremony for overworld crossings.

**5 · Dungeons stay discrete (for now).** The seamless model is for the **overworld**. Dungeon mouths
remain *entered* spaces (a deliberate threshold), which keeps the gate/mini-boss/boss setpiece intact
and bounds the scope. (A region's boss/gate can still gate *dungeon/depth* access; the overworld
itself is open to roam.)

**6 · Progression becomes roam-first.** The strictly linear "beat boss → next zone via the hub chain"
gives way to **free roam** across the contiguous overworld. Boss/gates gate *depth and dungeons*, not
the right to walk into the next field. **Exactly how/where roaming is gated (level-appropriate
borders, soft walls, story beats) is a design call to confirm with Dara** as it's built — flag, don't
invent a hard rule.

**7 · Save (ADR 0007) stores world position.** Resume drops you at your world (x,y); the save's
ID-based, version-tolerant design already covers this (store world coord + nearest region id).

## Consequences / trade-offs

- **This is the largest engine change yet — STAGED, not one shot.** Stage 1: the world-cartographer
  places the existing regions in world-space (contiguous, border-aligned, 8-way) + the consistency
  test — pure data, no engine change, no player-visible change. Stage 2: a **seamless 2-region proof**
  (stitch one border — e.g. Greenvale↔Silverwood — render both, walk across with no load + a biome
  blend + music cross-fade). Stage 3: roll the seamless renderer/movement out to all overworld
  regions; retire `loadZone` for overworld crossings; key encounters/music to position. Each stage
  ships green and reversible.
- **Layering (ADR 0005):** region placement + the world graph stay **pure data** (`data/zones.ts` /
  a `data/worldmap.ts`); the world-space camera, multi-region streaming, seam blending, and
  position-derived state live in `controllers/field.ts` (+ `game.ts` for run state); systems stay
  DOM-free. No new runtime deps; Canvas/DOM only; static-host + iOS-safe (viewport culling matters
  for perf).
- **Touches a lot:** `field.ts` (world-space render/move/stream/blend), `zones.ts` (world placement +
  border alignment), `game.ts` (continuous position, region-derived state, save), music + encounter
  selection (by position, with border blends). The discrete `loadZone`/hub-chain shrinks to
  dungeon-entry + (optionally) story beats.
- **Bounded by design:** dungeons stay discrete; seamless is overworld-only first. Multi-continent
  world-space and sea/ship travel are later.
