## The Five Powers (Attunements)

All magic and identity in Gaia flow from five fundamental Powers, each with a domain and its own
nature:

- **SOL** — Expansion · Light · Fire · Entropy
- **NOX** — Preservation · Cold · Darkness · Order · Anti-Entropy
- **ANIMA** — Life · Purpose · Evolution · Vitality
- **QUANTA** — Probability · Time · Observation · Possibility
- **UMBRAXIS** — Gravity · Spacetime · Singularities · Cosmic Structure

They form a cycle of dominance — each beats the next and is broken by the one before:

```
SOL → NOX → ANIMA → QUANTA → UMBRAXIS → SOL
```

The thematic logic: entropy breaks frozen order (SOL>NOX); preservation chokes growth (NOX>ANIMA);
life adapts around rigid math (ANIMA>QUANTA); observation collapses uncertainty (QUANTA>UMBRAXIS);
and gravity collapses light — stars into black holes (UMBRAXIS>SOL, the strongest matchup in the
ring). No Power is ever supreme or useless: each has one prey, one predator, two neutral matchups,
and its mirror.

## The Five Deities (canon, Dara)

Each of the five Powers (the Attunement civilizations) had a **leader** — together, the **Five
Deities**. Their **specific names are not yet known**; they will be **uncovered as the story
unfolds**. (They are the five of the "five-faction" framing — the **Sixth Deity** is the being or
state *beyond* them.)

## History — The Convergence, the Sundering & the Long Recovery (canon, Dara)

> New canon from Dara, captured here verbatim-in-spirit; not yet folded into the other sources.

The five Attunements were not myths — they were **five Type II Kardashev civilizations**, each
native to *our home galaxy*, the galaxy in which Gaia sits. Roughly **5,000 years ago** all five
**converged on Gaia** — a flashpoint where they met **for reasons unknown** — and four of them
**waged war**. **ANIMA did not significantly take part**; the other four (SOL, NOX, QUANTA,
UMBRAXIS) fought, and their war **Sundered the world**.

When it ended, all five **vanished from the planet** with no explanation, and **have not been heard
from since**. What they left behind is **heavy mana residue**, seeped deep into Gaia — and,
mysteriously, it has been **growing *more* potent across the 5,000 years since**, not fading.

Gaia already held a **highly advanced civilization** when the war came. It was **largely wiped
out**, and the world was thrown **back into the Stone Age**. The fractured survivors were scattered
and **isolated** across the overworld — and some into the underworld. Over the millennia,
civilization has **slowly regrown**: science is reawakening and technology advancing again,
especially as people **unearth the technology and knowledge of the lost civilization**.

Today, people know **something happened 5,000 years ago — but not what.** The truth of the
Convergence, the war, and the vanishing is lost to them.

*(This is why mortals remember the cataclysm as "the gods clashing": the Attunement civilizations
are perceived as deities, and the five-faction framing — Archons, the Sixth Deity — descends from
them.)*

## The Sundering — the broken heart of the world

The cataclysm above left **five divine scars** in the southeast — the only Attunement-themed
regions in all of Gaia, and the world's natural endgame:

| Scar | Epithet | Power |
|---|---|---|
| **Sol Scar** | The Radiant Expanse | SOL — scorched, blazing star-winds |
| **Nox Scar** | Eternal Dusk | NOX — frozen perpetual night, obsidian cities |
| **Anima Scar** | The Verdant Heart | ANIMA — violent, blessed overgrowth |
| **Quanta Scar** | The Fractured Continuum | QUANTA — broken time, impossible architecture |
| **Umbraxis Scar** | The Gravity Maw | UMBRAXIS — floating islands, gravity unbound |

## The world

Four continents around a central ocean, **The Great Expanse**, with a parallel **Underworld**
beneath:

- **Aurelion — The Heartland** (NW): the cradle of civilization, seat of humanity's greatest
  nations. The **starting continent** — Greenvale and the Duskmarsh are here.
- **Varkhaz — The Untamed Frontier** (NE): deserts, colossal jungles, volcanic badlands; only the
  strong or cunning survive.
- **Myr'Thalas — The Ancient Continent** (SW): oldest of the known lands, where empires "reached
  for the stars and vanished into time"; ruined, arcane, half-sinking.
- **The Sundering** (SE): the five scars above — the endgame.

## People & power — the path to godhood

A person's **Class** is set by the **weapon they wield**: an Attunement × one of nine Weapon
Archetypes (Sword & Shield, Dual Swords, Two-Handed Sword, Hammer, Dual Daggers, Dual Pistols,
Rifle, Staff, Spellblade) — **45 classes** in all. Change the weapon, change the calling. Each
class channels its Power through **MNA**, per-Attunement mana that forms a talent tree.

Mastery escalates toward the divine:

- **Soul Burn** — a warrior life-drains *themselves* to amplify their power past safe limits (a
  great healer can keep a "burning" ally alive far beyond reason).
- **Harmonic Ascension** — two harmonized souls of the same Attunement proc together to produce
  power greater than the sum of their parts.
- **Archon** — embodying a Power physically. *Type I*: mortal mastery of one Power. *Type II*: a
  living paradox mastering two at once. *Type III*: the mysterious path toward…
- **The Sixth Deity** — a being or state beyond the five-Power framework. **Deep, late-game lore**:
  we are a long way from uncovering it, but it is canon that it will **play a big role in the story
  much later.** Deliberately under-defined for now — "what lies beyond the game."

## The Present Age — the Scramble for Mana (canon, Dara)

> New canon from Dara. Carries a present-day **political/economic backdrop** that will play heavily
> in the background of the story.

With the Attunement civilizations gone and only their ever-strengthening **mana residue** left
behind, **mana is power** — pure and simple — and **people want it**. **Governments and factions
are scrambling to harness whatever mana sources they can to gain supremacy.** This rivalry runs
constantly beneath the world's surface, shaping nations, alliances, and conflict.

**Aether** is the **crystallized form of mana**, and it is what people **covet** — it serves as the
world's **currency**. Beyond buying and selling, Aether is a working material: at the **smith**, it
is spent **together with mana materials** (which drop from encounters) to **forge new weapons** or
to **modify existing ones**. *(Further crafting details to be delineated.)*

> **Design flags (gameplay — agent lane, to reconcile later):** the POC currently uses **gold** as
> currency and the smith is a placeholder. Reconciling toward this canon means **Aether as the
> currency** and a **smith crafting/modification loop** that consumes Aether + dropped mana
> materials. Not yet built — flagged for when the details land.

### The political shape of the Scramble (canon, Dara)

> New canon from Dara. Fills in *who* is scrambling for mana and *why the world feels dangerous*
> outside the player's own pocket of it — the background pressure the story sits inside.

Gaia is **vast** — powerful governments, powerful individuals, and powerful creatures all share it.
As mana's presence has grown stronger over the 5,000 years since the Convergence, mortal society has
climbed back up through **tribes → societies → nations**, and now the largest nations have hardened
into **empires**. It is a **mad dash across the planet** to acquire mana — power in its most raw
form — and different powers are at different points in that race: some only recently started
harvesting it as it's grown more abundant; others have been **quietly hoarding it for generations,
some for nearly a millennium.**

**Two empires are presently at war**, and the fallout is not contained to them — there will be
**collateral damage across Gaia**. Beneath and around that war move **rogue groups, mercenaries,
pirates, and every other kind of party thirsty for power** — and that's before counting the
**monsters** that inhabit the world in their own right. (Empire names and the specifics of the war
are **not yet known** — to be uncovered as the story unfolds, per the same pattern as the Five
Deities.)

## Where the playable story stands now

The game is an early vertical slice set in **Aurelion**:

- **Hearthford** — your lived-in starting village (inn, market, smith, shrine, townsfolk).
- **Greenvale** (Lv 1–6) — temperate farmland and forest beset by bandits; boss the Bandit
  Kingpin/Brute.
- **The Duskmarsh** and its dungeon **The Drowned Vault** (Lv 7–10) — boss the Cave Troll.

### Greenvale — where the heroes' story begins (canon, Dara)

Against that vast, empire-scale backdrop, **Greenvale is a small, isolated pocket of the world** —
far removed from governments and powerful figures, its very existence almost unknown outside its own
borders. It is near this quiet shire that **ancient ruins have recently been unearthed**, with a
**high concentration of mana emanating from within them**. This is where our heroes' journey starts.

> **Design flag (gameplay — agent lane, to reconcile later):** the ruins are a new geography/plot
> hook, not yet placed in Greenvale's built zone data or Areas (`data/zones.ts`) — the "Bandit
> Warren" dungeon and Bandit Kingpin arc are the current playable content. What the ruins actually
> *are* (a POI, a future dungeon, the seed of a later story beat) is Dara's to define when he's
> ready; flagged here so the level-design/world-building pipeline knows to reconcile toward it.

The grand systems above — Soul Burn, Ascension, Harmonic Ascension, the Archon path, and the
Sundering endgame — are **canon but not yet built**. The POC is the first slice of a far larger
world, to be reconciled toward REQUIEM over time.
