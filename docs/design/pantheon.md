# The Pantheon — The Five and the First Civilization

**Dara owns the world and lore; this records his canon.** This doc captures the cosmology behind the
"gods" referenced elsewhere in the lore (the Sundering's "where the gods clashed," the underworld's
"but the gods came first"). Anything beyond Dara's stated canon is marked **(proposed)** and stays
subordinate to his word. Use Gaia's vocabulary precisely (`CONTEXT.md`).

---

## Canon (Dara)

- **The "gods" of Gaia are the Five.** What older lore calls *gods* are in truth the **leaders /
  embodiments of the five Attunements**: **Sol, Nox, Anima, Quanta, and Umbraxis**. They are the
  **pantheon** — one being (or presence) per Power.
- **Each leads their own civilization and order.** Every Attunement is not just a force but a
  *people* — a civilization and an order that follows its Power, with one of the Five at its head.
- **One origin, then a fracture.** The Five all **originated from the same single civilization**.
  Over time that civilization **fragmented**, and from the fragments the Five rose — each forming
  **their own civilization and order** around their Attunement.
- **Their nature is unknown — deliberately.** What kind of life-forms the Five are is **a mystery
  and stays one**: beings of energy manifested as physical matter? pure energy? physical beings
  something else entirely? Canon does **not** answer this. The ambiguity is the point.

> In one line: *the Five are the pantheon — siblings of a single lost civilization that fractured
> into five Powers, each god now the head of their own Attunement's people. What they truly are,
> no one knows.*

## How this fits the existing canon

This slots cleanly into lore already on the books (see [`world-atlas.md`](world-atlas.md)):

- **The First Civilization → the fracture.** The underworld is the relic of a **pre-Calamity,
  space-faring civilization** — *"They built bodies, then minds… They built successors."* That single
  origin civilization is the one the Five fragmented out of. The Power-aligned **orders** are its
  splinters. *(The exact link — that the underworld's Forgotten Civilization IS the Five's shared
  birthplace — is a natural reading; **proposed**, for Dara to confirm or refine.)*
- **"But the gods came first."** The old world tried to build a final **AI** (Titan Core Prime) "to
  guide Gaia into a new age of creation" — *"but the gods came first."* Those gods are the Five:
  they pre-empted the machine successors with their own ascendance.
- **The Sundering = where the Five clashed.** The five **Sundering scars** — Sol Scar, Nox Scar,
  Anima Scar, Quanta Scar, Umbraxis Scar — are *"where the gods clashed."* Now read literally: each
  scar is the wound left by **that Attunement's god** in the Calamity. One scar per god, one god per
  Power — which is exactly why the scars are **the only Attunement-themed regions in the world**
  (world-atlas G4) and the game's natural endgame.
- **The affinity ring as a war of gods.** The ring (`affinity-ring.md`) — SOL→NOX→ANIMA→QUANTA→
  UMBRAXIS→SOL — reads as the eternal predator/prey order **among the Five themselves**; mortal
  combat just echoes their rivalry. *(Framing — proposed.)*

## The Five (reference)

Per-Attunement domains and **governing stats** (the stat their abilities scale from) are canon

**Legendary Figures vs. the Five.** The Five are gods/pantheon-scale. **Legendary Figures**
(`legendary-figures.md`) are singular mythic beings *below* that tier but above ordinary mortals —
e.g. The Last Lagrangian is an **Umbraxis** apex, an exemplar of that god's people, not the god.
*(Relationship — proposed.)*

## Item / writing hooks

- Relics and high gear can be **"of an order"** — Sol-order reliquaries, Nox-order war-steel,
  Anima-order seedwork, Quanta-order instruments, Umbraxis-order void-forge.
- **Artifact**-tier items can predate or stem from the **fracture** / the gods' clash (older than the
  Calamity — see the lore synopsis), or be **touched by a god** directly (a Scar relic).
- The Five's **unknown nature** is a flavor well: items "warm with a light that was never fire,"
  "humming with a presence that has no body," etc.
